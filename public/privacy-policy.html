Privacy Policy

Your data stays under your control.

DevFlowInc applications are designed to work without central application servers. We do not collect personal data from users, and we do not have access to private application databases stored in user-controlled accounts.

Effective date: March 15, 2026

1. Information We Do Not Collect

We do not collect, sell, rent, profile, or monetize personal data from users through our applications. We do not maintain central servers that receive or store user databases, documents, notes, bookmarks, or other private application content.

2. Local-First Architecture

Our products are built to run locally on the user's device whenever possible. Application data stays on the device or in storage locations chosen and controlled by the user. This architecture reduces the need for us to process or handle private user information.

3. Direct Google Account Connection

When an application offers synchronization or backup using Google services, the connection is made directly between the app and the user's own Google account. We do not proxy that traffic through DevFlowInc servers, and we do not keep a private copy of the application databases.

4. Access to Private Data

Because our services do not rely on central storage infrastructure for user databases, we do not have routine access to private data stored by users inside their apps. If a user grants permissions to Google APIs, those permissions apply to the user's account and are governed by the user's choices and by Google's own terms and privacy policies.

5. Analytics, Logs, and Tracking

We do not intentionally collect application-level user content, and we do not operate a behavioral advertising system based on private data. If a specific website or distribution platform uses basic technical logs needed for delivery or security, those logs are handled by the relevant platform provider, not as a central database operated by our apps.

6. Third-Party Services

Some apps may rely on third-party platforms such as Google for account authorization, storage, or synchronization. Those services are governed by their own legal terms, privacy notices, and security practices. Users should review those documents before enabling third-party integrations.

7. Security Responsibility

Since data is primarily stored locally or in user-managed accounts, users remain responsible for protecting their devices, passwords, Google accounts, backups, and permission settings. We recommend enabling strong authentication and reviewing connected-app permissions regularly.

8. Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy if our products, integrations, or legal obligations change. Any updated version will be published on this page with a revised effective date.
